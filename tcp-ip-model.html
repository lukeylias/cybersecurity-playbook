💡 OSI vs TCP/IP

OSI is the theoretical model with 7 layers — great for learning and understanding the distinct responsibilities of each network function. TCP/IP is the practical model with 4 layers — based on how the internet actually works. Both describe the same process, just grouped differently. In conversation, people use terms from both interchangeably. When you hear "Layer 3 switch" or "Layer 7 firewall," that's OSI terminology being used in practical contexts.

Key Points

🕑

TCP/IP came first. It was developed before OSI and is what the internet actually runs on. OSI was created later as a more detailed conceptual framework.

📚

OSI is more detailed. The 7-layer model is better for understanding specific responsibilities and for learning how network communication works at a granular level.

💬

"Layer 3 switch" or "Layer 7 firewall" — when you hear these terms, they're using OSI terminology. This is the standard for describing network device capabilities.

🔄

Terms are interchangeable. When someone says "Application layer" or "Transport layer," it could refer to either model. Context usually makes it clear.
